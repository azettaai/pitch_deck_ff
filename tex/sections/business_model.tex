\section{Business Model}

\subsection{Overview}

Azetta.ai delivers \textbf{Cosma}, an enterprise-grade omnilingual semantic search platform operating on a tiered subscription model with usage-based pricing. We target five distinct customer segments with differentiated value propositions and cost structures, generating revenue through three primary streams.

\subsection{Revenue Streams}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Model Usage Fees}: Consumption-based pricing for COEM embeddings
    \begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
        \item Dimension-based tiered pricing (see Section \ref{sec:coem-pricing})
        \item Bundled in subscription tiers or pay-as-you-go overage
    \end{itemize}
    
    \item \textbf{Managed Infrastructure}: At-cost pass-through pricing
    \begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
        \item \textit{Cloud Cost}: Customer pays raw cloud provider rate (e.g., AWS/GCP instance cost) with no markup.
        \item \textit{Billing}: Annual commitment preferred.
        \item \textit{Flexibility}: Zero-fee instance scaling (upgrade/downgrade) enabled by decoupled storage architecture.
    \end{itemize}
    
    \item \textbf{Professional Services}: Integration, training, custom models
    \begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
        \item Typical engagements: \$50K-\$250K
        \item Expected revenue mix: 10-15\% (Years 1-2), declining to 5\% (Year 5)
        \item Services include: Custom model training, integration support, enterprise onboarding
    \end{itemize}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Strategic Business Model Advantages}

Azetta.ai's pricing model is architected to create \textbf{sustainable competitive moats} while maximizing customer lifetime value:

\subsubsection{1. At-Cost Infrastructure Pass-Through}

\textbf{What it means:} Customers pay the raw cloud provider cost (AWS/GCP) for compute and storage with \textbf{zero markup}.

\textbf{Why this wins:}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{Radical Transparency}: No hidden margins or surprise infrastructure bills—customers see exactly what they're paying for
    \item \textbf{Competitive Immunity}: Competitors can't undercut us on infrastructure since we charge zero margin
    \item \textbf{Trust Builder}: Enterprise buyers love knowing we're not profiting from their infrastructure spend—builds long-term partnerships
    \item \textbf{Revenue Focus}: We make money on value-add (COEM models, search intelligence), not commodity infrastructure
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Customer impact:} A company spending \$5,000/month on infrastructure pays exactly \$5,000. With traditional vendors (20-40\% markup), the same workload costs \$6,000-\$7,000/month.

\subsubsection{2. Annual Commitments = Predictable Revenue}

\textbf{What it means:} Infrastructure costs are billed \textbf{annually in advance}, not month-to-month.

\textbf{Why this wins:}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{12-Month Cash Flow}: Every customer provides upfront capital, reducing working capital needs
    \item \textbf{Lower Churn Risk}: Annual contracts create switching friction—customers don't casually cancel mid-year
    \item \textbf{Financial Predictability}: ARR becomes highly predictable, making revenue forecasting accurate for investors
    \item \textbf{Customer Savings}: We pass along AWS annual commitment discounts (10-15\% savings vs. on-demand)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Investor impact:} By Year 3, 70\%+ of revenue is under annual contracts, providing \textbf{\$19.7M+ in prepaid cash} to fund operations without relying on external capital.

\subsubsection{3. Zero-Fee Scaling Flexibility}

\textbf{What it means:} Customers can scale infrastructure up or down \textbf{without change fees or penalties}, enabled by our decoupled storage architecture.

\textbf{Why this wins:}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{Eliminates Upgrade Friction}: Customers don't fear scaling up (no "gotcha" fees), accelerating expansion revenue
    \item \textbf{Technical Moat}: Decoupled architecture is hard to replicate—competitors use fixed instances
    \item \textbf{Expansion Revenue Driver}: Easy scaling drives 20-30\% annual upsells through natural usage growth
    \item \textbf{Risk Mitigation}: Customers can downgrade during slowdowns without penalty, reducing churn during economic downturns
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Economic impact:} Net Revenue Retention (NRR) of 110-130\% driven by frictionless expansion—every \$1 of new ARR becomes \$1.10-\$1.30 the following year.

\subsubsection{4. Hybrid Pay-As-You-Go + Subscription Model}

\textbf{What it means:} Customers buy bundled tiers (predictable base) + pay overage fees for usage spikes (consumption alignment).

\textbf{Why this wins:}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{Land-and-Expand}: Start with small subscription, grow into higher tiers as usage increases organically
    \item \textbf{Revenue Upside}: Overage fees capture unexpected growth (20-30\% of customers exceed base tier monthly)
    \item \textbf{Budget Certainty}: Base subscription is fixed; overage only applies when customers scale, aligning costs with their revenue growth
    \item \textbf{Self-Service Friendly}: Usage-based pricing removes sales friction—customers self-select appropriate tiers
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Revenue mix:} 60\% subscription (predictable) + 30\% infrastructure pass-through (annual) + 10\% overage/services (expansion) = highly diversified revenue base.

\subsubsection{5. Proprietary Ecosystem Lock-in}

\textbf{What it means:} The Cosma Vector Database (CVD) is tightly coupled with our model architecture.

\textbf{Why this wins:}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{Technical Dependency}: CVD's "zero-build" efficiency relies on specific vector properties produced only by Cosma models or our adaptation API.
    \item \textbf{No "Bring Your Own Vector" Leakage}: Customers cannot simply generate vectors with OpenAI and query CVD directly. They \textit{must} use our adaptation layer, ensuring we capture revenue even if they use third-party models.
    \item \textbf{Sticky Ecosystem}: The database and model reinforce each other—using one makes the other more valuable.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{6. Volume Expansion (The Jevons Paradox)}

\textbf{What it means:} By reducing the unit cost of vector search by 10x, we unlock massive-scale use cases that were previously cost-prohibitive.

\textbf{Why this wins:}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{New Workloads}: Instead of just indexing "hot" data (recent 10\%), customers can now afford to index \textit{everything} (archival data, logs, full history).
    \item \textbf{Competitive Defensibility}: Pass-through pricing + decoupled architecture = nearly impossible for competitors to match without rebuilding their entire platform
\end{itemize}
