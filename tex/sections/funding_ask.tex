\subsection{Capital Efficiency Strategy}

Azetta.ai is optimizing burn rate through:

\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{Lean Core Team}: Founders take below-market salaries (\$80K/year vs. \$150K+ market rate) to extend runway
    
    \item \textbf{Strategic Compute Spending}: Leverage spot instances and government research grants (Taha's NSERC connections) to reduce GPU costs by 40-60\%
    
    \item \textbf{Design Partner Model}: Early customers provide feedback and validation in exchange for discounted pricing, reducing CAC to near-zero for first 10 customers
    
    \item \textbf{Open-Source Leverage}: Release CVD core as open-source to drive developer adoption and community validation, reducing marketing costs
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.3cm}

Beyond these operational efficiencies, our business model itself is designed to create structural advantages that reduce risk and accelerate growth.

\subsection{Business Model Moats: Built-In Defensibility}

Beyond product differentiation, Azetta.ai's pricing architecture creates \textbf{structural competitive advantages} that de-risk this investment:

\subsubsection{1. Self-Funding Growth Engine}

\textbf{Annual infrastructure commitments} mean customers prepay 12 months of cloud costs upfront:

\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{Year 1}: \$375K in prepaid cash (30\% of \$1.25M ARR)
    \item \textbf{Year 2}: \$2.6M in prepaid cash (30\% of \$8.7M ARR)
    \item \textbf{Year 3}: \$8.5M in prepaid cash (30\% of \$28.2M ARR)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Investor impact}: By Year 3, we're generating \textbf{\$8.5M+ in upfront customer cash} annually—enough to self-fund operations with minimal equity dilution. This reduces Series B dependency and improves founder/investor alignment.

\subsubsection{2. Competitive Immunity from At-Cost Pass-Through}

Our \textbf{zero-markup infrastructure pricing} creates an impossible-to-beat moat:

\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item Competitors charge 20-40\% markup on infrastructure (\$6K-7K for workloads we charge \$5K for)
    \item \textbf{They cannot undercut us}—matching our pricing destroys their margins
    \item We profit from high-margin intelligence (COEM models), not commodity infrastructure
    \item Enterprise buyers value radical transparency—builds trust and long-term partnerships
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Defensive positioning}: Competitors must either (1) match our transparency and lose margin, or (2) maintain markups and lose customers. We win either way.

\subsubsection{3. Expansion Revenue Built Into Architecture}

\textbf{Zero-fee scaling} (enabled by decoupled storage) drives organic expansion:

\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item Customers upgrade \textbf{without penalty}—eliminates friction that kills upsells at competitors
    \item Target: \textbf{110-130\% Net Revenue Retention (NRR)} by Year 3
    \item 20-30\% of customers pay usage overage monthly (automatic expansion revenue)
    \item Can downgrade during downturns without penalty—reduces churn risk to <5\%
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Math}: Every \$1 of new ARR becomes \$1.10-\$1.30 the following year through natural expansion. This compounds: \$1M ARR → \$1.1M → \$1.21M → \$1.33M over 3 years \textit{with zero additional sales effort}.

\subsubsection{4. Technical Moat = Pricing Moat}

Our decoupled architecture isn't just a technical feature—it's a \textbf{pricing differentiator competitors can't match}:

\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item Traditional vector databases couple storage + compute, forcing customers into rigid instance tiers
    \item We decouple storage, enabling zero-fee scaling up/down
    \item \textbf{Competitors would need 12-18 months to rebuild their architecture} to match this capability
    \item First-mover advantage locks in customers before competitors can respond
\end{itemize}
